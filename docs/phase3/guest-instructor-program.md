# Guest Instructor Program

> How to recruit, onboard, prepare, and support guest instructors for the Vibe Coding Workshop.

---

## Overview

Guest instructors bring fresh perspectives, specialized expertise, and new energy to cohorts. They're typically:

- Experienced vibe coders with a specific skill or domain (design, finance, healthcare tech)
- Practitioners who teach well and have an authentic story
- Alumni who want to give back and have grown significantly since their cohort

**Phase 3 Goal:** Run at least 2 cohorts with a guest instructor or co-instructor by Spring 2027.

---

## Instructor Types

### Type 1 — Guest Segment Instructor
Leads one specific segment of the workshop (60–90 minutes) on their area of expertise.

**Examples:**
- A designer who teaches how to use v0 effectively for UI generation
- An API developer who teaches the Project 03 API section
- A founder who shares how they vibe-coded their real startup MVP

**Commitment:** 1 day (prep + delivery). No ongoing role.

### Type 2 — Co-Instructor
Co-leads an entire workshop day alongside Kyle. They share facilitation equally.

**When to use:** Virtual cohorts (easier to split facilitation), corporate training (two instructors for 10+ person groups), or when scaling to multiple simultaneous cohorts.

**Commitment:** 2 days (1 prep call + 1 workshop day).

### Type 3 — Lead Instructor
Leads a cohort independently using the full curriculum and materials.

**When to use:** Phase 4 goal — enables scaling without Kyle's direct involvement in every cohort.

**Commitment:** Full engagement: prep call, workshop day, post-workshop follow-up.

## Ideal Guest Instructor Profile

### Must Have
- Has built real apps using AI tools (can demo, not just talk)
- Comfortable speaking in front of 20+ people
- Warm, non-condescending communication style (matches the workshop brand)
- Can explain complex things with simple analogies
- Reliable and responsive

### Nice to Have
- Vibe Coding 1.0 alumni
- Has taught or mentored before
- Domain expertise relevant to a specific cohort audience (e.g., healthcare for a medical team)
- Active on social media (co-marketing opportunity)

### Not a Fit
- Gives lectures without hands-on demos
- Uses jargon without defining it
- Has a "you should know this" attitude
- Can't demo live (reliance on pre-recorded material only)

## Recruitment

### Source 1: Workshop Alumni
Best source. They've lived the experience and believe in it. Approach alumni who:
- Have been building consistently since their cohort
- Mentioned interest in teaching during the workshop
- Are active in the Discord community

### Source 2: Network Referrals
Ask the current community: "Who do you know that builds with AI and teaches well?"

### Source 3: Creator / Community Outreach
Identify creators or community members who:
- Write about vibe coding, AI tools, or building in public
- Have given talks at meetups or online events

## Selection Process

1. **Initial call (20 min):** Get to know them, share the workshop overview, answer questions
2. **Trial demo (15 min):** Ask them to explain one concept from their area (informal, no prep)
3. **Decision:** Within 48 hours of the demo call
4. **Offer:** Verbal first, then written agreement

## Onboarding Checklist

### 4 Weeks Before

- [ ] Sign instructor agreement (see template below)
- [ ] Share full curriculum package:
  - `docs/playbooks/README.md` (PB01 + relevant PBs)
  - `docs/guidebooks/README.md` (relevant guidebooks)
  - `docs/templates/README.md` (all project templates)
  - `docs/presentations/README.md` (relevant slide decks)
- [ ] Review and discuss any sections they'll own
- [ ] Schedule a prep call

### 2 Weeks Before

- [ ] Prep call (60 min):
  - Walk through the full day schedule together
  - Clarify who owns each segment
  - Discuss the participant audience (share persona profiles)
  - Review incident scenarios (PB03)
  - Q&A
- [ ] Give them read access to the GitHub repo
- [ ] Add them to the workshop Discord

### 1 Week Before

- [ ] Final check-in: "Any questions? Anything feel unclear?"
- [ ] Confirm logistics (venue, time, Zoom link for virtual)
- [ ] Review participant list together (share relevant personas)

### Day Of

- [ ] Arrive/join together (30 min before participants)
- [ ] Quick huddle: "Today's energy, today's contingencies"
- [ ] Agree on the signal for "hand off to you" (a nod, a phrase)

### After

- [ ] Debrief call (30 min): "What worked? What would you change?"
- [ ] Process compensation within 7 days
- [ ] Add to the guest instructor roster
- [ ] Invite to future engagements if applicable

## Instructor Agreement (Summary)

The full agreement is a short 1-page document covering:

1. **Engagement:** Date, format, role (segment / co / lead)
2. **Compensation:** Amount and payment timeline
3. **IP:** Workshop materials remain owned by Vibe Coding Workshop. Instructors may reference the workshop in their own portfolio/bio.
4. **Brand:** Instructors represent the brand during the engagement. No disparaging content about participants.
5. **Confidentiality:** Participant information is private.
6. **Photo/Recording:** Permission to use photos and recordings from the workshop for marketing (with individual participant consent following standard workshop policy).

## Scaling to Lead Instructors (Phase 4)

Phase 4 goal: Train 2–3 lead instructors who can run cohorts independently. This requires:

1. Guest instructor → Co-instructor → Lead instructor progression
2. A formal instructor certification: attend 1 cohort as observer, assist in 1 cohort, lead 1 cohort with oversight
3. Quality rubric: what does a well-delivered cohort look like?
4. Revenue share model that makes it worth their time

This is a Phase 4 build. Document the rubric after running the first co-instructor cohort.
