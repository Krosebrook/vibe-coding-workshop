# University Partnerships — Bringing Vibe Coding to Campus

> A program to integrate AI-assisted development into university curricula — through guest lectures, workshop pilots, and curriculum licensing.

---

## Vision

Thousands of students graduate every year without knowing how to build software. The university partnership program puts vibe coding in front of students who wouldn't self-select into a paid workshop — including students in non-CS programs who could benefit most.

## Guest Lecture Format

### 90-Minute Guest Lecture: "Build Anything with AI"

**Designed for:** Any 100–400 level course where students will benefit from understanding AI-assisted development.

**Agenda:**

| Time | Content |
|------|---------|
| 0:00–0:10 | Who I am, why vibe coding matters |
| 0:10–0:25 | Live demo: build a personal landing page in 10 minutes |
| 0:25–0:40 | What students can build with these tools (examples) |
| 0:40–1:00 | Hands-on: everyone opens Claude and builds one thing |
| 1:00–1:15 | Q&A: "Can AI replace [their profession]?" |
| 1:15–1:30 | Workshop overview, discount code for next cohort |

**What We Provide:**
- Slide deck (shareable PDF)
- Follow-up resource email for all attendees
- Discount code for next public workshop cohort

**Requirements from University:**
- 25+ students
- Projector or large display
- WiFi (tested before session)
- Email list to send follow-up resources

## Workshop Pilot Format

### Full-Day Workshop on Campus

Same as the Grayslake workshop, run on-site at the university.

**What We Provide:**
- Full workshop facilitation (Kyle or certified instructor)
- All curriculum materials and templates
- Project starter files
- Post-workshop resource kit for participants

**Requirements from University:**
- Lab or classroom with individual computers or BYOD policy
- WiFi capable of 20 simultaneous development environments
- $500 facility fee (covers instructor travel + materials)
- A faculty liaison to handle student registration

**Ideal Cohort:** 15–25 students

## Curriculum Integration

For courses that want to integrate vibe coding over a semester:

### Curriculum Module Package

| Module | Length | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| M01: Introduction to Vibe Coding | 2 hours | What it is, how it works, first prompt |
| M02: Building with Claude | 3 hours | Prompt patterns, iterative refinement |
| M03: Front-End Fundamentals | 4 hours | HTML, CSS, JS through the vibe coding lens |
| M04: APIs and Data | 3 hours | Connecting to external data sources |
| M05: AI Integration | 4 hours | Building AI-powered features |
| M06: Deploy and Ship | 2 hours | Vercel deployment, custom domains |

**Total:** 18 instructional hours across a semester

**Licensing Fee:** $1,500/semester for up to 30 students. Includes curriculum materials, instructor support Slack, and updates.

### What the University Owns
- The right to teach the modules for the licensed term
- All student work produced in the course

### What We Retain
- Curriculum IP
- Trademark on "Vibe Coding Workshop"
- Right to update curriculum and provide updated versions

## Partnership Process

### Step 1: Introduction
- Faculty member or department chair reaches out via email
- 30-minute call to understand fit and goals

### Step 2: Proposal
- We send a one-page proposal: format, cost, logistics
- Includes a sample module outline and references

### Step 3: Pilot
- One guest lecture or workshop pilot before signing a licensing deal
- Both parties evaluate fit

### Step 4: Agreement
- Sign the University Partnership Agreement
- Transfer first semester payment
- Schedule kickoff call

### Step 5: Delivery
- Deliver modules per schedule
- Mid-semester check-in with faculty liaison
- End-of-semester feedback review

## FAQ

**Q: Do students need their own laptops?**  
A: Yes. A computer lab with internet access works fine.

**Q: What if we can't install software on university machines?**  
A: Claude and v0 are browser-based. Cursor requires installation. We have a browser-only track in development.

**Q: Is this for CS majors only?**  
A: No — and ideally not. Non-CS students often get the most out of it.

**Q: Can you do this virtually?**  
A: Yes. The guest lecture and many modules can be run over Zoom. Full workshops are possible with some format adjustments.
