# Regional Workshops — Expanding Vibe Coding to Other Cities

> A framework for bringing the Vibe Coding Workshop experience to new cities — with consistent quality, local instructors, and the same "build by feel" ethos.

---

## Vision

The first workshop happened in Grayslake, IL. The goal for Phase 4 is to have workshops running in 3+ cities by end of 2027. Not franchises. Not a packaged product sold at a distance. Real workshops, run by instructors who believe in the mission, supported by centralized curriculum and tools.

---

## Expansion Criteria

Before entering a new city, we validate:

| Criterion | Threshold | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Local demand signal | 30+ waitlist sign-ups from city | Proves there's a community before we invest |
| Instructor identified | 1 confirmed local instructor | Workshop quality depends on the person in the room |
| Venue available | 1 confirmed venue, 20-seat capacity | No virtual workaround for the in-person energy |
| Tech infrastructure | Reliable WiFi + projector | Non-negotiable for a coding workshop |

## The Regional Instructor Program

### What Is a Regional Instructor?

A regional instructor is someone who:
- Has completed the Vibe Coding Workshop (alumni)
- Understands vibe coding from the inside — not just the theory
- Wants to teach it in their city
- Is willing to follow the curriculum and quality standards

They're not employees. They're certified partners.

### Certification Process

**Step 1: Application**
- Submit: city, background, why you want to teach this
- 15-minute video call with Kyle
- Review of past teaching/mentoring experience

**Step 2: Preparation**
- Complete the Instructor Guidebook (G22)
- Shadow one full workshop day (Grayslake or virtual)
- Run one pilot workshop with 5–8 participants (friends/colleagues OK)
- Submit pilot feedback and a video recording of one session

**Step 3: Certification**
- Review call with Kyle
- Pass checklist review of pilot workshop
- Sign the Regional Instructor Agreement
- Receive access to the Instructor Resource Kit

### What Certified Instructors Get

| Resource | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Curriculum license | Full workshop curriculum, slides, templates |
| Vibe Starter access | Early access to CLI tool for participant setup |
| Instructor Slack channel | Direct line to Kyle and other certified instructors |
| Co-marketing | Listed on vibecodingworkshop.com/instructors |
| Feedback tools | Participant survey templates and analytics |
| Onboarding kit | Printable checklists, name tags, handouts |

### Revenue Model for Regional Instructors

Regional instructors set their own pricing. The suggested range is $150–$300/participant (matching Grayslake pricing). No royalties are charged in year one. Starting in year two, a 10% licensing fee applies to cohorts above 15 participants.

## Curriculum Standards

All regional workshops must:

1. **Include all 4 projects** — The 4-project arc is the product.
2. **Use the approved tool stack** — Cursor, Claude, v0, Vercel, Supabase
3. **Follow the workshop day structure** — Morning foundations, afternoon projects, end-of-day deploy
4. **Use official templates** — From `docs/templates/` (regional instructors can suggest additions)
5. **Collect participant feedback** — Using the official post-workshop survey
6. **Never require coding experience** — "Beginner-friendly" is non-negotiable

### What Instructors Can Customize

| Customizable | Not Customizable |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Workshop name (can add city) | Core 4 projects |
| Local venue and date | Vibe Coding brand mark usage |
| Pricing | Tool stack |
| Catering | Curriculum structure |
| Examples and context | "Zero experience required" positioning |

## Quality Assurance

- **Participant survey** required after every cohort (NPS + open text)
- **Minimum NPS score:** 60 to maintain certification
- **Annual recertification** call with Kyle
- **Curriculum updates** pushed to all instructors via GitHub notification

## Virtual Workshop Format

Virtual is not a compromise — it's a different experience with its own strengths.

### Format Differences (Virtual)

| Element | In-Person | Virtual |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Platform | Cursor (local) | Cursor (local) + Zoom |
| Support | Walk around the room | Screen-share in breakout rooms |
| Energy | Room energy | Discord chat energy |
| Ship It ceremony | Shout out in the room | Post URLs in Zoom chat |
| Max participants | 20 (limited by room) | 30 (limited by instructor bandwidth) |

### Virtual Requirements
- Zoom Business or equivalent (breakout rooms required)
- Discord server for async support during the day
- Loom video guides for tool setup (prerecorded)
- 2-minute "I'm stuck" form for quick escalation
